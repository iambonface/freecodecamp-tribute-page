Martin

---- The peoples Watchman ----

1932

Joseph Martin Shikuku,Son of Oyondi is born. His mother was from Aba-Kisa sub tribe (also Aba-shisa) while his father Johana Oyondi was from Aba-Marama. Both were Luhya and peasant farmers.

Next

1953

He joins Railways as one of the first Africans to be trained as trained guard, prior to working as a Laboratory Assistant. At Magadi, he earned 80 shillings per month.[ USD. 0.80]

Back Next

1959

Serves as Secretary General, Nairobi Peoples Convention Party until 1963

Back Next

1960

Shikuku joins the Lancaster House conference delegation that helped negotiate✓ Kenya’s independence.

Back Next

1961

He pursues elective politics and lost. During an interview by Robert M. Press in 2002, he claimed that he won lost the election because he had only campaigned for about 2 weeks.

Back Next

1963

He is elected to parliament, Served from 1963 – 1969; during which he was appointed Assistant Minister in the Office of the Vice-President and Home Affairs by the late President Jomo Kenyatta.

Back Next

1969

Serves his second term in Parliament before his detention in October 15, 1975. Apparently he was detained because he said the then government party was “dead”.[sic] He was detained without trial for 3 years, 1 month and 12 days.

Back Next

1978

He is released and elected again reelected to parliament serving from 1979 to 1983, and 1983 to 1988..

Back Next

1990

Son of oyondi starts opposition to government and in 1991, Forum For Restoration of Democracy (FORD) party came to the pipeline. Same year he failed to acquire political rally license and decided to hold Kamukunji rally instead.

Back Next

1997

Shikuku parts ways with Matiba after a disagreement.  He forms Ford-Asili and was its Presidential Candidate. He lost his presidential bid and could not secure the parliamentary seat. His party won only one parliamentary

Back Next

2012

Shikuku succumbs to prostate cancer while receiving treatment at the Texas Cancer Centre, Nairobi.

May his soul Rest In Peace!
